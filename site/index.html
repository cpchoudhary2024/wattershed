Method in one paragraph

Each site is screened on three pillars scored 0–100 (higher = more concern): water stress (WRI Aqueduct 4.0 baseline water stress 50% + five-year county drought climatology 30% + current U.S. Drought Monitor category 20%), grid strain & carbon (eGRID2023 subregion CO₂e-rate percentile 60% + NERC 2025 LTRA resource-adequacy category 40%), and cumulative community burden (a CalEnviroScreen-style pollution × vulnerability index over 16 tract indicators, ranked against all 85,396 U.S. census tracts). Pillars are deliberately not averaged into one number; transparent trigger rules assign the tier, and modeled demand can escalate it. Full methodology, sensitivity analysis, and limitations are in the repository.

The U.S. Drought Monitor is jointly produced by the National Drought Mitigation Center at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, the United States Department of Agriculture, and the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration.

Honest limits: tract statistics describe areas, not parcels; eGRID rates are annual averages, not marginal emissions; county water denominators are 2015 vintage; five pollution indicators are frozen at EJScreen 2.32; demand figures are engineering estimates, not measurements.
